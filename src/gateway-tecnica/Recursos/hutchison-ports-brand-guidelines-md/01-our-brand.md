## Introducción (página 3)

# THESE ARE THE HUTCHISON PORTS BRAND GUIDELINES.

The contents of this document should be applied as consistently as possible across our network's branded materials, communications and infrastructure.

Consistency across the world will reflect our strength as one true global network.

These guidelines are also flexible, and designed to accommodate the needs of our individual Business Units – so that we can continue to communicate our local and regional strengths and build on their equity.

## Our Tone of Voice (página 8)

To keep things simple, we've distilled our tone of voice down to three words. Whenever we communicate – externally or internally, in emails, adverts, or in-person – we are clear, confident and committed.

### CLEAR
We communicate with government ministers, marine professionals, importers, forwarders, unions and workers. While the context of these conversations is often very different, the way we speak should always be the same – clear and simple. This way, our message will always be heard, and our audience will understand and engage.

### CONFIDENT
As leaders, we look forward, not back. We steer our own course, without focusing on our competitors. We are confident not arrogant; we let our achievements speak for themselves. And because our business is based on trust, we're open and transparent – even if something goes wrong.

### COMMITTED
We're totally committed to our customers and partners. Our relationships lie at the heart of our success. So we speak with purpose, without forcing ourselves on others. Striking the right balance will impress our current partners and customers – and help us attract new ones.

### Guiding Principles

**Keep it simple.** Short sentences and common words are easy to understand.

**Make the most of formatting.** Use bullet points, subheadings and paragraph breaks to aid legibility.

**Clear doesn't mean boring.** Focus on the reader benefit, use intrigue to draw people in.

**Use positive language.** 'You can do X' – and focus on benefits and opportunities.

**Use active, direct language.** 'We are building a new port,' is more confident than 'A new port is being built.'

**Keep it relevant.** Look to simplify things. Only say what's necessary or useful for the reader. Avoid repetition.

**Don't be arrogant.** There's a difference between boasting and being confident.

**Our audience comes first.** Speak with their needs in mind. Try to understand the person you're talking to. Is there a particular topic they want to talk about or focus on?

**Be friendly.** Use warm, personal but professional language.

**Be flexible and understanding.** Our business brings us into contact with a huge range of people. They all have different needs. Never make assumptions about them.
