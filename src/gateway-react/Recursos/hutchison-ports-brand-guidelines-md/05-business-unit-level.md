## Typography (página 102)

For consistency, individual Business Units use the same typeface as our group brand.

This typeface is **Verlag**. Please refer to page 39 of this document for more guidance.

## Business Unit Naming in Copy (página 109)

To create a strong global identity for the Hutchison Ports brand it is important that the newly agreed BU names are communicated in a consistent fashion across written applications. Please ensure the use of the old BU names are avoided and the following guidelines are adhered too.

### Where there is a country/place name
When a country or place name is used for a BU name (eg. Ajman, Tanzania, Thailand, Sohar, Pakistan, etc.), it must always be prefixed by **"Hutchison Ports"** in order for it to make sense and contextualise it. Note that Hutchison Ports + BU place name should always be written in title case.

1. In the first instance always use the full name, within written communication, i.e. **Hutchison Ports + BU name**.
2. Thereafter, use "We", "the port", "the terminal(s)", "the facility", etc.

> **Ejemplo:** *Hutchison Ports Sohar is an ultra-modern container-handling facility capable of accommodating the latest generation of mega-vessels. The terminal is located outside the Straits of Hormuz in the Gulf of Oman, approximately 200 kilometres from Muscat and…*

### Where there is an acronym or a standalone name
When the name of the BU makes sense on its own (eg. Port of Felixstowe) or is an acronym (eg. ECT, BEST, ICAVE), there is less need to prefix it with Hutchison Ports on every mention. Ideally the first instance of the BU name in a document would reference the full name, including the Hutchison Ports prefix, but subsequent references may use the BU name in isolation. Standalone names should be set in title case, while Acronyms should always be set in upper case.

1. In the first instance always use the full name, within written communication, i.e. **Hutchison Ports + BU name**.
2. Thereafter, use "the acronym", "the terminal(s)" or "the port".

> **Ejemplo:** *Hutchison Ports BEST, is the first semi-automated terminal of Hutchison Ports and the most technologically advanced port … BEST is capable of serving multiple mega-vessels simultaneously …*

## Business Unit Rules on Abbreviation (página 110)

Our network is known as **Hutchison Ports**. This name should be used in all contexts (with the one exception highlighted below). Our name should always be written and spoken in full, never abbreviated. This rule applies to all forms of communication, including email addresses, URLs and body copy.

The **only exception** to the rules above concerns how our company is referred to in a legal context when the full name of our holding company is used; **Hutchison Port Holdings Limited**.

## Business Unit Corporate Mark Usage — tamaño y posición (página 111)

How to determine the size and positioning of our Corporate Mark.

### Determine Logo Size
For most formats, **divide the length of the diagonal (Y) by 35.** This gives the height of the Corporate Mark (X).

> **Example:** A4 portrait: Y = 364 mm; X = 364 / 35 = **10.4 mm**.

### Position
Always positioned **top left**; the distance between the Corporate Mark and the margins is dictated by the exclusion zone principles (clear space = **1.5 Z**). The margin between the Corporate Mark and the edge of the page also defines the margins on the grid.

Excepciones (format-specific / common sense):
- Centred on TV and video endframes
- Bottom right on advertising (as a sign-off)

## Building Signs (páginas 112–114)

### Branded vs Unbranded signage (página 112)
On site, we use our Corporate Mark with consideration. Overusing it makes our brand feel automatic and ultimately undermines our visual identity.

Use the Corporate Mark or the logo only on signs that have a **non-generic name** (eg. Tomline House). **Don't** use the logo on generic signs (eg. "Fire & Ambulance", "Toilets", "Stores and Purchasing").

- **Branded signage** (ejemplos): "PORT OF FELIXSTOWE — TOMLINE HOUSE", "[logo] WICKENDEN HOUSE".
- **Unbranded signage** (ejemplos): "FIRE & AMBULANCE SERVICE", "STORES & PURCHASING".

### Balanced Sign (página 113)
How to determine the size and positioning of our brand on existing signs and fascias. To maximise impact the clear space rules differ from those given on page 13.

In many cases, existing sign boards and fascias will need to be utilised for the new brand. This example shows how to use our Corporate Mark on signage, while introducing the name of a building.

The sign name cap height equals the height of the logo mark. The logo mark height (shown as 'x') is defined as **half the total height of the sign board**. The clear space top and bottom is **half the height of the logo**.

> **Note:** In all cases the sign or fascia backing should be in a clean and serviceable condition before applying the new brand.

### Centred Signs (página 114)
In cases where there is no branding, the same principles to find the cap height are used as the branded version. The Capital "H" height (shown as 'x') helps keep the proportions harmonious across the site. The sign name is centred across the whole width of the sign area.

Proporciones marcadas: clear space superior e inferior = 0.5x; altura de capital = x; separación entre dos líneas = 0.2x.

## Using the Logo On-site (página 117)

When using the logo on-site, we have to consider the size of the logo in relation to where it is applied in order to create the most impact, while maintaining our considered, elegant style.

The logo is always **centered, vertically and horizontally, and presented on white**.

### General Rules
Exclusion zone rules apply to most on-site applications. Note that awkward shapes are managed (la zona de exclusión se ajusta a la forma irregular de la superficie).

### Exceptions
In some instances, i.e. on the side of buildings, the exclusion zone can be challenged in order to manage impact and legibility from afar. As a general rule, the direct space around the logo should never feel smaller than the surface of the logo.

- ✅ Correcto: con espacio alrededor.
- ❌ Incorrecto: el logo demasiado ajustado al borde.

The rules on this page don't apply to applications where the size of the logo is key to make it visible, or where impact is our priority. For example, painted logo on roofs of buildings.

## Business Units: On-site Heavy Machinery (páginas 118–130)

### Introducción (página 118)
On-site stakeholder visits and press coverage on business units sites, give our brand a unique opportunity to be featured and associated directly with what we do day-to-day. Heavy machinery branding is a key touch-point for Hutchison Ports to increase visibility across all business units.

The following pages offer guidance on how to apply our brand to on-site heavy machinery.

### Principles — Logo or full Corporate Mark? (página 119)
Our logo must have maximum impact when it is to be seen from afar, or from off the ground. Engine room covers on quay cranes will be branded with the **logo**; it is optional to show Mid-level crane beams with the **BU full corporate mark**.

In some instances, such as operational vehicles that have only a limited space to display the full corporate mark, we will resort to use the logo as a shorthand for our brand.

### Position and Size (página 120)
The Network or Business Units' corporate marks should be **centred** whenever possible.

We will always try to centre our logo both horizontally and vertically on any areas that it is applied to. Use your judgement to visually center it on asymmetric spaces.

For defining the size of the logo, use the exclusion zone defined in the appropriate logo sections of these guidelines.

- **On defined parts:** Centered (default) / Visually centered (exception).
- **On beams:** Centered (default) / Centered within available space / left-aligned (to give way to more important information).

### Quay Crane logo application on Engine Room (páginas 121–122)
How to determine the size and positioning of our logo on large scale exterior executions. El ejemplo muestra la colocación del logo en la M-House (engine room).

**Colores (ver página 21 para equipment colour standards):**
- Logo Sky Blue: **RAL 5012**
- White: **RAL 9016**
- Logo Sea Blue / azul marino: **RAL 5002**

**Position:** Optically align the logo to the centre of the crane's engine hub.

**Scale:** Use the exclusion zone and clear space rules when defining the scale of the logo. This helps our logo feel surrounded by the right amount of clearspace and maintains visual consistency across applications on port sites.

> **Note:** the faint tinted sky-blue shapes around the logo are only to guide usage, and are **not** to be reproduced on the engine room.

**DO NOT (con "X" roja):**
- Do not make the logo too big on the given available surface.
- Do not use the full Corporate Mark. It would appear too small from the ground.
- Do not use our graphic pattern. Our logo is the single most compact element to represent our brand.

### Quay Crane Corporate Mark application on Beam (páginas 123–124)
This is how our Corporate Mark appears on large scale exterior applications. Standard placement is the default for when the area that the Corporate Mark appears on is clear of other visual elements.

In exceptional cases, where an element will visually obstruct the area the Mark is applied onto, we will visually centre the Mark.

**Colores:** White: RAL 9016 / azul marino: RAL 5002.

- **Standard Placement:** alinear el centro de la marca con el centro de la viga (horizontal y verticalmente).
- **Visually Aligned Placement:** usar cuando un elemento obstructor bloquea un área de la viga; centrar el logo en el área despejada.

**Scale:** Use the exclusion zone and clear space rules when defining the scale of the logo.

**DO NOT (con "X" roja):**
- Make sure the mark's scale and position respect the exclusion zone defined around the corporate mark.
- Do not position the mark using the usage rules defined for print.
- Do not adapt the mark's colours, in part or completely.

## Corporate Mark Application on Equipment (páginas 131–132)

In general, all equipment should follow the below colour examples across the network.

The logo should be shown on all **major scale equipment** such as quay side cranes and yard cranes. Always apply **reverse white logo** unless the background colour is white (eg. Quay Crane M-house).

Smaller scale equipment such as transfer cranes do not require to show the Hutchison Ports Corporate Mark or logo. These equipment painted with previous Corporate Mark may either debrand or replace with the appropriate logo.

Public transportation vehicles should always use Corporate Mark in full colour when against a white background or reverse white against a dark background (eg. coach, pick-up truck, etc.).

Following the heavy machinery branding principles in these guidelines will create consistency across the network and from site to site. However we understand that specific challenges might apply locally. The important thing is to keep consistency within the same type of machinery.

> **When in doubt, please contact GCA for further assistance.**

Tipos de equipo ilustrados (con la zona de aplicación marcada): **QC** (Quay Crane), **RMGC** (Rail Mounted Gantry Crane), **RTGC** (Rubber Tyred Gantry Crane), **Straddle Carrier**, **Reachstacker**, **Empty Container Handler**, **Mobile Harbour Crane**, **Coach**, **Pick-up Truck**.

Versiones de control remoto (página 132): **Remote Control QC** y **Remote Control RTGC**.
