## Our Logo (página 10)

Part of our new Corporate Mark is a logo of our position as the world's leading port network.

El logo se construye con cuatro conceptos:

- **Colour blocks.** The two blue colour blocks – one light, one dark – represent the sky and the sea.
- **The angle.** Importantly, the white line points upwards in order to represent Hutchison Ports' focus on growth, progress, innovation and ambition.
- **The space.** This represents the horizon, which connects sky and sea.
- **The whole.** Our logo represents a union of assets, strengths and values that make up the world's leading port network.

## Corporate Mark Usage (página 17)

How to determine the size and positioning of our Corporate Mark.

### Determine Logo Size
For most formats, **divide the length of the diagonal (Y) by 35.** This will give you the height of the Corporate Mark (X).

> **Example:**
> A4 portrait:
> Y = 364 mm
> X = 364 / 35 = **10.4 mm**

### Position
Always positioned **top left**, the distance between the Corporate Mark and the margins is dictated by the exclusion zone principles. The margin between the Corporate Mark and the edge of the page also defines the gutters of the grid (clear space = **1.5 Z**).

The only exceptions to this rule are on specific applications where format-specific conventions and common sense dictate. For example:

- Centred on moving image endframes
- Bottom right on advertising (as a sign-off)

## Corporate Mark Don'ts (página 19)

Our Corporate Mark represents us, and everything we do. It helps build and reflect our reputation. We should therefore make sure that it is presented consistently and in its best form at all times.

Here are some examples of what to avoid (cada uno marcado con una "X" roja):

- **Don't** stretch, rotate or distort the Corporate Mark.
- **Don't** use effects or add textures to the Corporate Mark.
- **Don't** change any of the colours.
- **Don't** reorganise the Corporate Mark.
- **Don't** use Corporate Mark and logo on the same visible side of the same printed application.
- **Don't** place the Corporate Mark over complex backgrounds or imagery.
- **Don't** add elements to the Corporate Mark.
- **Don't** use transparencies.

## Shapes: Don'ts (página 31)

Whilst they lend flexibility to our appearance, our graphic shapes must be used in a coherent way. Here are a few things to avoid (cada uno con "X" roja):

- **Don't** use Sky and Sea shapes on the same page.
- **Don't** use multiple Sky and/or Sea shapes on the same page.
- **Don't** use shapes at extreme scales.
- **Don't** use transparency or filters.
- **Don't** use the shapes at opposite angles.
- **Don't** crop in a way that creates full-bleed stripes.
- **Don't** use shapes that aren't either alongside each other, or overlapping.
- **Don't** use effects like shadow.
- **Don't** overlap images in related shapes.
- **Don't** use multiple images within the same shape.
- **Don't** use the shapes as outlines.

### Photography: Don'ts (página 38)

Our image style reflects our view on the world, as well as the standards we judge ourselves by. Here are a few things to avoid (cada uno con "X" roja):

- **Don't** portray infrastructures in a bad light.
- **Don't** use cut-out images.
- **Don't** use awkward or expected crops.
- **Don't** use blurred or hazy images.
- **Don't** use flat, abstract shots.
- **Don't** use unnatural perspectives.
- **Don't** show ambivalent attitudes when showing people.
- **Don't** use 'posed' or contrived images.
- **Don't** use forced compositions.
- **Don't** hide or obscure subject matter.
- **Don't** use CGI or artificially-composed images.

## Typesetting Principles (página 43)

Just as tone of voice is important in upholding our brand and values, good typesetting is crucial to communicating clearly and confidently.

Below is an initial guide for how we set our type. However, there will be specific instances where a distinct approach to type will need to be taken – for example in digital communications like email.

**Type should always be ranged left.**

Escala tipográfica de referencia (tamaño/interlineado y tracking):

| Tamaño / Interlineado | Tracking |
|-----------------------|----------|
| 24/28 pt | -5 |

## Typography Hierarchy (página 44)

Typographic hierarchy helps deliver relevant information quickly and effectively. Below are some basic guides to follow.

- **Verlag Black** is used for large headlines as well as small headlines and subheads and numerical details, but not for body copy.
- **Verlag Light** should only be used as body copy.
- Colour can be used to enhance the hierarchy – however body copy should always appear in **Pantone 294**.

### About punctuation
As a rule, we punctuate all copy; including headlines, subheads, body copy and pull-out quotes.

The exceptions are for 'Titles' – like the one which sits above the headline. A title is usually something which introduces a page, a list, or a document.

Reglas de jerarquía mostradas:
- **Descriptor and secondary titles:** Verlag Black.
- **Headline:** Verlag Black on two lines.
- **Subheads:** in all caps Verlag Black, highlight colour.
- **Body copy:** Verlag Book. *Italics* and **Bold** can be used to add typographic detailing.
- **Pull-out quotes:** colour can be used for pull-out quotes and body copy.

## International Typography (página 45)

Localisation of type is best implemented by local designers who have an understanding of both design and culture in the relevant market. This will ensure consistency in style and meaning.

### English
- **Headline** is set in Black, all caps. (Verlag)
- **Sub-headers** are set in Book, all capitals.
- Alternativa con **Montserrat (Google Fonts):** Headline en Bold all caps; sub-headers en Regular all capitals.

### Chinese (example)
- Tipografía: **Noto Sans CJK TC (Google Fonts)** – pesos Thin, Light, Demilight, Regular, Medium and Bold.
- Due to the nature of Chinese typefaces, the text can appear relatively bold and blocky. Adding **+75 tracking** to the line spacing can address this.
- It is common practice to adjust the size of Chinese typefaces to be **10% smaller** than English typefaces, especially when they are being used together. This will result in a similar x-height.

## Which Chinese Typeface? (página 46)

When setting text in Chinese, we revert to **Monotype Hei HKS** for Simplified Chinese, and **Monotype Hei HK** for Traditional Chinese, where Noto Sans can't be used.

### Simplified Chinese
- **Corporate / Web-Multimedia typeface:** Noto Sans SC (思源黑体).
- **Default system typeface:** Monotype Hei HKS (正黑体). MHei HKS replaces Noto Sans in digital applications for Simplified Chinese.

### Traditional Chinese
- **Corporate / Web-Multimedia typeface:** Noto Sans TC (思源黑體).
- **Default system typeface:** Monotype Hei HK (正黑體). MHei HK replaces Noto Sans in digital applications for Traditional Chinese.

Notas:
- Noto Sans (思源黑體) is a free Google font and can be installed on any PC/Mac. Free to use commercially; it exists in two versions and bridges traditional and simplified Chinese.
- Both MHei HKS and MHei HK are default system typefaces pre-installed on most Microsoft systems. They should only be used where necessary – for instance internal documents, digital applications and HTML email.
